What to Expect

Your therapist will arrive 10-15 minutes prior to your scheduled session to conduct your intake and set up the massage table with fresh sanitized linens. You will need a 7' by 10' clear space for the table and room for the therapist to move around it. Please make sure any excitable pets are taken care of. While we love your furry companions they are not licensed for tandem massage or impromptu table leg construction. Therapists need access to a place to wash their hands and step out of the room while you get comfortable on the table under the top sheet and comforter. Top sheets are always required in accordance with our professional code of conduct and licensing standards. You will be professionally draped at all times. If you have any question or would like the therapist to adjust the pressure at any time please speak up and let them know. Please see our Policy and Terms of Service prior to booking.

Post Massage Care

You may be achy or light headed after a massage especially if it has been an extended time between sessions. This is common and normal. Massage puts strain on the musculature similar to a light work out and treating your body with care after a session can help you get the most out of your massage. Staying hydrated and paying attention to your posture and tension can help with lasting effects of massage. If you are sore or uncomfortable after a session the use of heat and gentle stretching can help your body recover. If this is a post-injury massage ice can also be helpful. Both should be used in moderation.
